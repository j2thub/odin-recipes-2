Homemade Pretzel

A soft, warm pretzel is a delicious reminder of my study abroad experience in Munich, Germany. In the states, it's hard to find one that compares to the large, golden-brown bread found in my favorite city in Europe. Today, we are going to learn how to make an authentic recreation of the traditional bavarian beer-snack.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon barley malt syrup or light brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups (355 grams) warm water
  • 5 cups (635 grams) all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 teaspoon fine salt

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400Â°F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. Spray with nonstick cooking spray or grease with butter.
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the dough hook, combine the yeast, barley malt syrup/sugar, and 1/4 cup of the water. Whisk to combine. Let stand for 5 minutes or until frothy.
